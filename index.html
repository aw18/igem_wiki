Welcome to the 2014 UBC iGEM Wiki!

Project Overview: As demand for British Columbia's minerals grow in the global markets, so does the B.C. mining industry. However, current mining practices are expensive, chemically intensive and energetically demanding. These practices lead to aesthetic challenges (open pits) and the generation of tailings ponds that are difficult to monitor and control in the future.
2014 UBC iGEM project seeks to improve the efficiency of mineral separaton from impurities via a bacterium expressing two types of peptides on its surface.
